Overview

Build the retention engine
before you scale acquisition

Based on our discovery conversation, BARTR is well-positioned with strong fundamentals. The Uber integration is live, and the platform appeals to individual users, thrift stores, and small business owners. Clear demand and user traction are still being established, and that is exactly why the immediate priority should not be acquisition. Before scaling the top of the funnel, BARTR needs to ensure the users who do arrive stay, transact, and come back.

Three immediate opportunities stand out as the highest-leverage investments Tropoly can help deliver: automating user engagement communications, redesigning the step-by-step transaction experience, and building a competition-ready pitch deck for Web Summit. The first two are designed to keep users engaged and build the habits that drive retention. The third ensures BARTR shows up at Web Summit with a story that lands.

Project 01

Engagement Automation

A behavioural email and push notification system that re-engages browsers, surfaces relevant items, and keeps BARTR top of mind, without manual effort from your team.

Project 02

Transaction Experience Design

A step-by-step interface and communication flow that guides both buyers and sellers through every stage of a trade, reducing confusion, drop-off, and support overhead.

Project 03

Pitch Deck

A competition-ready pitch deck for Web Summit's Startup Program, built to tell BARTR's story with clarity, confidence, and visual impact.

Looking Ahead

Phase 2 Opportunity

AI-powered product listing, in collaboration with a specialized product development partner

Beyond the three immediate projects, a larger opportunity exists around the product upload experience itself. The current one-at-a-time listing process is a meaningful barrier to growth, particularly as BARTR moves into a storage service model and begins attracting thrift stores and small business owners who need to list inventory at scale.

The vision: a user pans a video camera across a rack of items. The system identifies each product, pulls market pricing, assesses condition, and creates individual listings, automatically. This is technically feasible today using video frame separation, image recognition via the Gemini API, and market pricing lookup via SERP API.

01

Proof of concept

Tropoly can scope and build a functional POC to validate the core video-to-listing workflow, demonstrating the concept before any significant development investment.

02

Production development

Full production-ready development would involve a specialized product development partner working alongside Tropoly, keeping marketing and product aligned from day one and eliminating the cost of siloed vendor work.

03

Market differentiation

Solving the upload problem is not just a product improvement. It is a marketing story. It creates meaningful separation from Poshmark, Facebook Marketplace, and every other peer-to-peer resale platform competing for the same users.

Scope note: Phase 2 is not included in this proposal. It is offered as context for the broader opportunity ahead. If you would like to explore it, we are happy to discuss what a discovery and scoping engagement for this work would look like.
